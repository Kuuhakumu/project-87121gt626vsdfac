# Career-Match Quiz

> 12 questions, ~2 minutes. Grab paper or open a notes app and tally your letters. There are no wrong answers — answer how you *actually* are, not how you think you *should* be. Honest answers get honest results.

**How to score:** For each question, write down the letter(s) of your answer. At the end, count how many of each letter you picked. Your top letters point to your best-fit fields.

---

### 1. A free Saturday and no obligations. You'd rather…
- **A.** Build something — a gadget, a spreadsheet system, a little app
- **B.** Take apart how something works or figure out how it could be broken
- **C.** Organize a messy system until it runs smoothly
- **D.** Dig into a pile of data/info to find the interesting pattern
- **E.** Help a friend fix their tech problem and explain it to them
- **F.** Redesign something clunky to make it nicer to use
- **G.** Plan an event — coordinating people, budget, and timeline
- **H.** Figure out what a group *actually needs* and rally everyone around the plan

### 2. Which compliment would mean the most?
- **A.** "You built something really clever."
- **B.** "You spotted the flaw nobody else saw."
- **C.** "Everything you set up just *works*, reliably."
- **D.** "Your analysis completely changed our decision."
- **E.** "You're the person everyone goes to for help."
- **F.** "This is so intuitive and pleasant to use."
- **G.** "You kept the whole team organized and on track."
- **H.** "You always know what we should build next, and why."

### 3. How do you feel about writing code?
- **A.** Love it / want it to be the core of my job
- **B.** Happy to script and use code as a tool
- **C.** Some scripting is fine, not all day
- **D.** SQL and a bit of Python, sure
- **E.** A little is fine; I prefer people problems
- **F.** I'd rather not; design/strategy over code
- **G.** Not for me
- **H.** A little — enough to talk to engineers; SQL is useful

### 4. How do you feel about math/statistics?
- **A.** Fine with logic; don't need heavy math
- **B.** Comfortable enough
- **C.** Keep it light
- **D.** I actually enjoy stats and numbers
- **E.** Light, please
- **F.** Light
- **G.** Just budgets and basic ROI
- **H.** Some — metrics, A/B tests, prioritization scoring

### 5. Your ideal amount of human interaction at work?
- **A.** Small team, heads-down focus time
- **B.** Small focused team
- **C.** Some — mostly systems, some people
- **D.** Medium — stakeholders + focus time
- **E.** High — helping people all day
- **F.** High — collaborating with users and teams
- **G.** High — leading and coordinating people
- **H.** High — constant cross-team communication

### 6. A system breaks at a bad time. Your instinct?
- **A.** Dig into the code to find the bug
- **B.** Ask "was this an attack? how?"
- **C.** Restore service fast, then prevent it recurring
- **D.** Check what the data/logs say happened
- **E.** Calm the affected people while you fix it
- **F.** Ask "why did the design let this happen to users?"
- **G.** Coordinate the response and comms
- **H.** Ask "what's the user impact, and what do we tell them?"

### 7. Which problem sounds most satisfying to solve?
- **A.** Make this app feature work end to end
- **B.** Find out how an intruder got in
- **C.** Make deployments automatic and bulletproof
- **D.** Figure out why sales dropped last quarter
- **E.** Get this user's broken laptop working again
- **F.** Make this confusing checkout flow simple
- **G.** Get this delayed project back on schedule
- **H.** Decide which feature to build next and why

### 8. How remote-friendly do you need your work to be?
- **A/D/F.** Strongly prefer remote-friendly
- **C.** Prefer remote
- **B/G.** Flexible — hybrid is fine
- **E.** Fine being on-site / in-person

*(This one doesn't score letters — it's a reminder: Support is often on-site; Dev/Cloud/Data/UX Design are the most remote-friendly. PM is remote-friendly but relationship-heavy, so often hybrid.)*

### 9. Money vs. speed-to-first-job: what matters more *right now*?
- **E.** I want into the industry as fast as possible
- **A/D/F.** I'll invest time to target the field I want
- **B/C.** I'm okay doing a stepping-stone role first
- **G/H.** I'm thinking long-term/leadership

### 10. Pick the toolset that sounds most fun:
- **A.** A code editor, Git, a terminal
- **B.** Kali Linux, Wireshark, a SIEM dashboard
- **C.** AWS console, Terraform, Docker
- **D.** SQL, Power BI / Tableau, Python notebooks
- **E.** Ticketing system, hardware, remote-support tools
- **F.** Figma, user interviews, prototypes
- **G.** Roadmaps, budgets, project boards
- **H.** Notion, analytics dashboards, PRDs

### 11. Which downside could you tolerate best?
- **A.** Constantly learning new frameworks
- **B.** Shift work / not truly entry-level
- **C.** On-call at 3am sometimes
- **D.** Cleaning messy data, stakeholder requests
- **E.** Lower starting pay, repetitive tickets
- **F.** Very competitive; portfolio takes time
- **G.** Needs prior experience first
- **H.** Not a true first job; need an on-ramp role first

### 12. In five years you'd love to be…
- **A.** A senior engineer building core products
- **B.** A security specialist (defender or ethical hacker)
- **C.** A cloud/infrastructure engineer
- **D.** A data scientist or analytics lead
- **E.** A sysadmin / network engineer (or springboarded into security/cloud)
- **F.** A senior product designer or UX lead
- **G.** An IT manager or director
- **H.** A product manager shaping what gets built

### Reading your result

- **Clear winner (one letter dominates):** Start there. Skim its guide's intro to confirm the day-to-day sounds right.
- **Two-way tie:** Read both fields in [what-is-each-field.md](what-is-each-field.md). Pick the one whose *day-to-day* and *downsides* you can live with.
- **Lots of B, C, G, or H but you have zero experience:** Those are usually **second** jobs (Product Management and IT Management especially are destinations, not starting points). Consider **E (IT Support)** as a fast on-ramp, then pivot. See the "realistic first jobs" table in [what-is-each-field.md](what-is-each-field.md#quick-reality-check-which-are-realistic-first-jobs).
- **Lots of E:** Good news — it's the most beginner-friendly way into the industry.

> **Still unsure?** That's fine. Everyone does the same [Shared Foundations](foundations.md) (Hours 0–40) regardless of field. Start there — you'll pick up enough about computers, the CLI, and how the internet works to make the choice clear. You lose nothing.
