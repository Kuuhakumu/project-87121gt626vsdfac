## the honest progression

```text
APM / PM I: 0-2 yrs
  -> PM II / PM: owns a product area end to end, 2-4 yrs
  -> Senior PM: complex product, cross-team dependencies, 4-7 yrs
  -> Staff PM / Principal PM: company-wide product strategy
     OR Group PM / PM Lead: manages PMs, 7-10 yrs
  -> Director of Product
  -> VP of Product / CPO
```

most PMs eventually choose between:

- [ ] **IC track:** go deeper on product craft and strategy.
- [ ] **management track:** build and run a team.

the choice usually becomes real at the Senior -> Staff/Group PM fork.

## what each level adds

### APM -> PM

- [ ] move from executing a plan to owning discovery for your area
- [ ] stop asking "what should i build?" and start asking "what problem is worth solving and why now?"
- [ ] ship things that fail and learn from them
- [ ] become willing to kill your own ideas when evidence says to

### PM -> Senior PM

- [ ] **ambiguity tolerance:** run discovery when the right answer is unknown.
- [ ] **influence at scale:** stakeholders include execs, legal, and cross-functional leads.
- [ ] **setting goals:** define OKRs for your area, not just execute them.
- [ ] **technical credibility:** push back on estimates or architecture with enough understanding to be useful.
- [ ] **mentoring:** help junior PMs before u have a formal lead title.

### Senior PM -> Group PM / PM Lead

- [ ] own a portfolio of products or product areas
- [ ] hire, onboard, and develop junior PMs
- [ ] set product strategy for a domain
- [ ] connect team OKRs to company objectives
- [ ] represent product in executive conversations

### Group PM -> Director -> VP/CPO

CPO work is different from senior IC PM work.
its organizational design, resource allocation, culture-setting, and executive partnership.
much more leadership than product craft.

most CPOs have 10-15 years across multiple companies.
this level needs strong writing, speaking, credibility, and product bets that paid off.

## senior specializations

senior PMs often combine product domain knowledge with functional depth.

| Compound specialization | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Senior Growth PM | runs experiment platform and growth OKR framework |
| Senior Technical PM | owns developer-facing API platform with deep engineering partnership |
| Senior B2B PM | owns enterprise tier, renewals, expansion, enterprise-specific features |
| Senior AI PM | leads LLM-powered product line, model evaluation, safety review, AI roadmap |

## AI impact on PM careers

this is directional, not a measured statistic.
verify current conditions when u are in market.

- [ ] **tactical Product Owner work is increasingly automated.** LLMs can draft user stories, acceptance criteria, release notes, and feedback summaries.
- [ ] **discovery and strategy are more durable.** deciding what to build, synthesizing ambiguity, and making hard tradeoff cases still require judgment.
- [ ] **AI PM is a real emerging specialization.** companies need PMs who understand evaluation, non-deterministic outputs, responsible AI, and model drift.
- [ ] **practical implication:** use AI for drafts and summaries, but invest in discovery craft, user empathy, judgment, and communication.

## skills that compound

| Skill | Why it pays off |
|---|---|
| **Writing** | async orgs run on written alignment. |
| **Product sense** | sharpens with every product u study deeply. |
| **Data fluency** | SQL + analytics make u self-sufficient. |
| **Engineering partnership** | trusted PMs get better estimates, faster builds, earlier warnings. |
| **Storytelling** | strategy docs, roadmap pitches, exec decks depend on framing. |
| **Domain knowledge** | 5 years deep in fintech matters more to fintech than 5 years hopping verticals. |

## how to keep growing

- [ ] write publicly: teardowns, case studies, frameworks
- [ ] read Lenny's Newsletter, SVPG essays, First Round Review
- [ ] do discovery on yourself every 6-12 months: what shipped, did it matter, what did u avoid?
- [ ] find a senior PM mentor through ADPList with specific questions
- [ ] ask for harder scope: ambiguous projects, cross-team initiatives, hard stakeholders

PM is a role built on judgment, and judgment takes time.
frameworks are scaffolding while intuition develops.
by year 5, the frameworks fade into the background, and whats left is a PM who can enter a new product area and ask the right questions.
