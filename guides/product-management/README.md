> [!IMPORTANT]
> **read this first: Product Management is not a true entry-level field.**
> even postings labeled "Junior PM" routinely ask for 2-5 years of relevant experience.
> PMs need earned credibility with engineers, designers, and executives. that comes from shipped work and judgment, not just studying frameworks.
>
> realistic on-ramps: APM / RPM programs, internal transfer from engineering/design/data/sales, PM-adjacent roles, startups where u can own a surface area faster, or MBA pipelines at companies that recruit PMs that way.
>
> treat this as a **6-18 month project**, not a 3-month sprint. dw, the path exists. its just not magic.

## how to track your progress meow

the `- [ ]` boxes in this guide are display-only in this public repo.
u cant actually tick them here unless u commit to our repo, which is not your personal tracker ;w;

**use one of these instead:**
- **fork this repo** and tick boxes in your fork.
- **copy the checklist into your notes** in Notion, Obsidian, OneNote, or whatever u already use.
- **download or print it** if physical tracking helps.

the checkbox only shows the path.
the real progress signal is the artifact: a PRD, teardown, roadmap, metrics case, interview answer, or real on-ramp action u can defend.

## how the hour system works

timelines are in **study hours**, not weeks, so they work at any pace.

| Your pace | 500 hours takes |
|---|---|
| 1 hr/day | ~17 months |
| 2 hrs/day | ~8 months |
| 4 hrs/day | ~4 months |
| 6 hrs/day (full-time) | ~3 months |

each goal shows an approximate hour band.
thats a budget, not a deadline.
at the default **2 h/day**, the study path is roughly **8 months**, while the on-ramp work may run longer.

## certification reality in 2026

```text
[Entry signal]    PSPO I / CAPM: cheap, shows u did the work
[Scrum shops]     CSPO / PSM I: useful where Scrum vocabulary matters
[Enterprise/gov]  PMP: real gatekeeping power, needs experience
[B2B/SaaS]        Pragmatic Institute PMC: recognized in Pragmatic Framework shops
[Skip]            Product School cert / AIPMM CPM: low employer recognition in tech
```

in tech and startups, certs are mostly ignored.
product sense and shipped artifacts win.
use certs strategically, not as a substitute for a portfolio.
see [certifications.md](certifications.md) before paying.

## what makes this guide different

- **honest about the entry door** - no fake "zero-to-PM in 3 months" promise.
- **kills the CEO-of-product myth** - PMs have influence without authority.
- **separates PM from Product Owner** - ticket-writing is part of the job, not the whole job.
- **portfolio = thinking artifacts** - PRDs, teardowns, prioritization docs, metrics cases.
- **hour-based** - fits any schedule.
- **verified June 2026** - tool pricing, cert costs, and APM program status checked in research notes.
- **region-agnostic** - no salary tables, no local job-board lists.
- **free-first** - Notion, Jira free tier, Amplitude free tier, GA4, DataLemur.
