## start-here picks

- [ ] **Visual fundamentals:** *Refactoring UI* by Adam Wathan and Steve Schoger. practical UI-design book for people learning by doing.
- [ ] **UX theory:** *Don't Make Me Think* by Steve Krug. read this early, about 3 hours.
- [ ] **Practice:** [Daily UI](https://www.dailyui.co) + [Sharpen.design](https://sharpen.design). repetition builds the hand.
- [ ] **Portfolio feedback:** [ADPList](https://adplist.org). free 1:1 sessions with working designers and researchers.
- [ ] **Community:** [r/UXDesign](https://www.reddit.com/r/UXDesign/). honest feedback and job-search reality checks.

## books worth owning

| Book | For |
|---|---|
| *Don't Make Me Think* by Steve Krug | everyone. foundational usability book. start here. |
| *The Design of Everyday Things* by Don Norman | everyone. the mental model for UX as a discipline. |
| *Refactoring UI* by Adam Wathan and Steve Schoger | UI/visual design. practical guide to making interfaces look right. |
| *100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People* by Susan Weinschenk | psychology applied to design decisions. |

u dont need all of these at once.
start with Krug and Wathan/Schoger in Goal 1.
Norman and Weinschenk deepen research and critique skills from Goal 2 onward.

## stay current

- [ ] **Figma Blog** (figma.com/blog) - product updates, Config content, design system thinking.
- [ ] **Nielsen Norman Group Articles** (nngroup.com/articles) - research-backed UX articles, free and high quality.
- [ ] **IxDF Articles** (interaction-design.org/literature) - topic-based reading lists, free to browse.
- [ ] **r/UXDesign weekly threads** - market signal. filter for portfolio feedback and job hunting.

on AI tools: in 2026 youre expected to know generative design tools exist, like Figma AI, Framer AI, and Midjourney for moodboards.
honest take: they speed up visual exploration.
they dont replace judgment about what to design or why.
researchers and strategists are least threatened; visual production work is most assisted.
