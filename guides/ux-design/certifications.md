## TL;DR — What to actually get

**In UX, a portfolio is the real filter. Certs appear in fewer than 10% of job postings.** Most hiring managers at product companies don't ask about them — the exceptions are government/regulated-sector roles (where formal credentials matter more) and career-switcher keyword screens on ATS systems.

The cheapest sensible path: **build 3 case studies first**, then optionally add the Google UX cert (~$294) for ATS keywords, then an IxDF membership (~$156/yr) for ongoing structured learning. Skip NN/g until you're a senior designer or moving into consulting — it costs ~$6,400 and most junior-level hiring managers won't weigh it heavily.

- **Build the portfolio first.** Certs without portfolio case studies do not get you hired.
- **Google UX Design Certificate:** most useful as an ATS keyword and a structured self-study framework — not a strong hiring signal with experienced UX teams.
- **IxDF membership:** best ongoing-learning investment. 40+ courses, per-course certificates, ~$156/yr.
- **NN/g UX Certification:** prestigious but expensive (~$6,400). Skip until senior or consulting.
- **Figma has NO cert.** The cert page 404s. Don't list "Figma Certified" on a resume.
- **Adobe Certified Professional** = tool cert for Creative Cloud, not a UX-practice signal. Skip for UX roles.
- **Adobe XD is discontinued.** Do not study it, list it, or mention it as a current tool.

## Employer signal ranking

🔴 **Baseline (ATS keyword value for career switchers):**
- **Google UX Design Certificate** — recognized in applicant tracking systems; signals self-directed commitment. Experienced UX teams tend to treat it skeptically, but it can get you past an initial keyword filter.

🟠 **Strong differentiator (for the right context):**
- **IxDF membership + per-course certs** — signals ongoing structured learning across research, IA, usability, and interaction design. 40+ courses means you can tailor to your track.

🟡 **Nice-to-have / track-specific:**
- **NN/g UX Certification (UXC)** — genuinely respected but priced for consultants and senior practitioners. Not expected at junior level; strong signal if you're repositioning into UX consulting or a senior/specialist role.

⛔ **Skip:**
- **Adobe Certified Professional** — tool cert, not a UX-practice signal. Irrelevant for product design roles.
- **Figma certification** — does not exist (404).
- **NN/g UXMC** — ~$19,300. Leadership-tier only. Not a first-decade cert.
- **Any bootcamp graduation cert** — bootcamp completion alone is not a differentiator. The portfolio is.

## Recommended paths

**Cheapest → first UX job (free → ~$294):**
Build 3 case studies (free) → optional Google UX Design Certificate (~$294 on Coursera, financial aid = effectively free) to cover keyword screens → publish portfolio.

**Best ongoing learning investment (~$156/yr):**
IxDF membership. Take courses aligned to your track: "User Research," "Information Architecture," "Interaction Design," "Design Thinking" in year one. Add more as you advance.

**Senior / consulting tier (skip until experienced):**
NN/g UX Certification (~$6,400) — worth pursuing if you're targeting a research-specialist, senior designer, or UX consulting position. Not worth the cost at junior level.

**Government / regulated-sector path:**
Google UX cert + IxDF membership covers most formal requirements at entry level. Check the specific posting — some government roles specify NN/g or UXMC.
