## TL;DR: what to actually get

in UX, a portfolio is the real filter.
certs appear in fewer than 10% of job postings.
most product-company hiring managers dont ask about them.
the exceptions are government or regulated-sector roles, and ATS screens where career switchers benefit from keywords.

cheapest sensible path:

1. build 3 strong case studies first
2. optionally add the Google UX cert (~$294) for ATS keywords
3. optionally use IxDF membership (~$156/yr) for ongoing structured learning
4. skip NN/g until senior or consulting

- [ ] **Portfolio first:** certs without case studies do not get u hired.
- [ ] **Google UX Design Certificate:** useful as an ATS keyword and structured self-study framework, not a strong signal to experienced UX teams.
- [ ] **IxDF membership:** best ongoing-learning investment. 40+ courses, per-course certificates, about $156/yr.
- [ ] **NN/g UX Certification:** respected but expensive, around $6,400. skip until senior or consulting.
- [ ] **Figma has no cert:** dont list "Figma Certified" on a resume.
- [ ] **Adobe Certified Professional:** tool cert for Creative Cloud, not a UX-practice signal.
- [ ] **Adobe XD is discontinued:** dont study it, list it, or mention it as current.

## employer signal ranking

### baseline: ATS keyword value for career switchers

- [ ] **Google UX Design Certificate** - recognized by applicant tracking systems. experienced UX teams may treat it skeptically, but it can help a resume screen.

### useful for the right context

- [ ] **IxDF membership + per-course certs** - signals ongoing structured learning across research, IA, usability, and interaction design.

### nice-to-have / track-specific

- [ ] **NN/g UX Certification (UXC)** - genuinely respected, but priced for consultants and senior practitioners. not expected at junior level.

### skip

- [ ] **Adobe Certified Professional** - tool cert, not UX practice.
- [ ] **Figma certification** - does not exist.
- [ ] **NN/g UXMC** - about $19,300. leadership-tier only.
- [ ] **Any bootcamp graduation cert** - completion alone is not a differentiator. the portfolio is.

## recommended paths

**cheapest -> first UX job (free -> ~$294):**
build 3 case studies, optionally take Google UX Design Certificate on Coursera for keyword screens, then publish the portfolio.

**best ongoing learning investment (~$156/yr):**
IxDF membership.
take courses aligned to your track: User Research, Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Design Thinking.

**senior / consulting tier:**
NN/g UX Certification (~$6,400) can be worth it for research-specialist, senior designer, or UX consulting roles.
not worth it at junior level.

**government / regulated-sector path:**
Google UX cert + IxDF membership covers most formal entry-level requirements.
check the exact posting, bc some government roles specify NN/g or UXMC.
