## why specialize now

Goals 1 and 2 gave u the shared foundation.
but IT Management & Strategy splits into five tracks with different frameworks, certs, daily work, and destinations.
u cant go deep on all five at once.
hiring managers want a clear lane.

| Track | Core question | Key framework | Typical destination |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ITSM** | are our services running well and improving? | ITIL 4 | Service Delivery Manager |
| **Project / Program** | are we delivering this change successfully? | PMP / PRINCE2 | PMO Director |
| **Governance / Strategy** | is IT serving the org and managing risk? | COBIT / TOGAF | CIO / Enterprise Architect |
| **People / Eng Management** | is the team healthy and growing? | leadership craft | Director of Engineering |
| **IT Operations** | is everything up, fast, and secure? | ITIL + SRE | Head of Infrastructure |

pick the row whose question u want to own.
read all five once, then commit your hours to one.

## track 1: IT service management

**u will like this if:** process, predictability, and continual improvement feel satisfying.
its turning chaos into a reliable service.

### go deep on this

- [ ] full ITIL 4 practice set beyond incident/problem/change
- [ ] service value chain and four dimensions applied to a real service
- [ ] service catalog and service portfolio design
- [ ] CMDB concepts and why configuration data enables safer change
- [ ] continual improvement register, not just firefighting

**certs that fit:** ITIL 4 Foundation or ITIL Foundation v5, then ITIL Specialist: Create, Deliver & Support, then ITIL Managing Professional.

**senior reality:** Service Delivery Manager / IT Service Manager.
u own service-desk health, major incidents, vendor SLAs, continual-improvement backlog, dashboards, and QBRs.

### labs

| Lab | Platform | What u do | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full service catalog | Notion or Confluence | design 10 services with owners, SLAs, request paths, costs | **Free** |
| ITSM workflows | ServiceNow Developer Instance | configure request, incident, and change workflows with SLA timers | **Free** |
| Continual-improvement register | Notion | log 10 improvements, prioritize, track 3 to done | **Free** |

**practice gate:** u can walk through the ITIL 4 service value chain on a real service and explain how configuration data enables safe change management.

## track 2: IT project & program management

**u will like this if:** deadlines, defined outcomes, and shipping complex change give u energy.

### go deep on this

- [ ] full delivery methodology: PMBOK or PRINCE2
- [ ] program management across related projects
- [ ] portfolio prioritization: MoSCoW, weighted scoring, funding decisions
- [ ] earned value management: cost and schedule variance
- [ ] Agile at scale: SAFe or Scrum-of-Scrums
- [ ] benefits realization after delivery

**certs that fit:** CAPM -> PMP, or PRINCE2 Foundation -> Practitioner. PSM I if running agile delivery.

**senior reality:** Senior PM -> Program Manager -> PMO Director.
u run steering committees and connect IT delivery to business sponsors.

### labs

| Lab | Platform | What u do | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Multi-stage project plan | Asana or Jira | plan an infrastructure migration with milestones, dependencies, critical path | **Free** |
| Portfolio prioritization model | Google Sheets | score 8 candidate projects and recommend which 4 to fund | **Free** |
| Google PM Certificate audit | Coursera | audit planning, execution, Agile modules and keep artifacts | **Free** audit |

**practice gate:** u can choose waterfall vs Agile for a project and explain how earned value reveals trouble before deadline slip.

## track 3: IT governance & strategy

**u will like this if:** big-picture questions pull u in: IT spend, business goals, enterprise risk, technology capability.

### go deep on this

- [ ] COBIT 2019: governance vs management, goals cascade, maturity, risk appetite
- [ ] TOGAF: ADM, capability architecture, IT structure serving strategy
- [ ] enterprise IT risk management
- [ ] multi-year IT strategy and roadmapping
- [ ] compliance and audit readiness
- [ ] SFIA as capability-planning tool for the IT function

**certs that fit:** COBIT Foundation -> CGEIT or CRISC. TOGAF Foundation -> Practitioner for architecture. CISM if governance includes security.

**senior reality:** IT Governance Lead -> Enterprise Architect -> IT Director -> CIO.
u report risk/value to leadership, own multi-year roadmap, and make build-vs-buy or invest-vs-retire calls.

### labs

| Lab | Platform | What u do | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| COBIT 2019 intro | [ISACA COBIT resources](https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit) | study governance/management split and goals cascade | **Free** intro |
| TOGAF ADM overview | [The Open Group TOGAF](https://www.opengroup.org/togaf) | read ADM overview, sketch capability architecture | **Free** overview |
| 12-month IT roadmap | Miro or Linear | themed roadmap with rationale and dependencies | **Free** |

**practice gate:** u can explain COBIT governance vs management and sketch how architecture connects a business goal to IT capabilities.

## track 4: people & engineering management

**u will like this if:** growing people and building a healthy team feels better than any individual technical win.

### go deep on this

- [ ] IC -> manager transition
- [ ] effective 1:1s
- [ ] performance management, growth plans, underperformance
- [ ] hiring: role definitions, structured interviews, bias reduction
- [ ] team structure and topology
- [ ] delegation and prioritization
- [ ] psychological safety and accountability

reading list is the curriculum: *The Manager's Path*, *An Elegant Puzzle*, *Staff Engineer*, plus Lethain.com.

**certs that fit:** almost none carry real weight.
PSM I or ITIL Foundation can show process literacy, but management ability is demonstrated, not certified.

**senior reality:** Engineering/IT Manager -> Senior Manager -> Director.
1:1s, hiring loops, coordination, and removing obstacles become the job.
u write little code or config, and thats the point.

### labs

| Lab | Platform | What u do | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:1 framework | Notion or Google Docs | design structure, recurring questions, growth-theme tracking | **Free** |
| Role definition + rubric | Google Docs | job description and structured interview rubric for one IT role | **Free** |
| Team health check | Atlassian Team Playbook | run Health Monitor on a fictional team, identify top 2 interventions | **Free** |

**practice gate:** u can describe the IC->manager shift and explain how u would structure a 1:1 and fair hiring loop.

## track 5: IT operations

**u will like this if:** keeping systems reliable, fast, and secure feels satisfying.

### go deep on this

- [ ] infrastructure and platform management
- [ ] monitoring and observability
- [ ] SRE practices: SLIs, SLOs, error budgets, toil reduction
- [ ] incident command at scale
- [ ] capacity and availability management
- [ ] disaster recovery and business continuity: RTO/RPO, DR testing, backups
- [ ] security operations basics: patching, vulnerability management

this track sits close to [IT Support & Networking](../it-support-networking/) and [Cloud & DevOps](../cloud-devops/).
the difference: u manage people, process, budget, and SLAs, not the hands-on engineering itself.

**certs that fit:** ITIL 4 Foundation for service/ops process; vendor ops certs in Cloud/IT Support guides for technical depth.

**senior reality:** Infrastructure Manager -> Head of Platform / Head of Infrastructure.
u own uptime, on-call, capacity, DR strategy, and infrastructure budget.

### labs

| Lab | Platform | What u do | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| On-call program | PagerDuty free | escalation policy, rotation, alert rules, major-incident roles | **Free** |
| SRE workbook | [Google SRE books](https://sre.google/books/) | read SLO/toil chapters and define SLOs - ⚠️ TODO verify exact chapter links | **Free** |
| DR plan outline | Notion or Google Docs | define RTO/RPO for 3 systems and backup/restore strategy | **Free** |

**practice gate:** u can design on-call escalation, define SLOs with error budget, and explain RTO vs RPO in a DR plan.

## how to choose

u are not signing a lifetime contract.
but for the next ~100 hours and your Goal 4 portfolio, pick one track and commit.

- [ ] most accessible entry on-ramp: ITSM and IT Operations.
- [ ] clearest credential ladder: Project/Program and Governance.
- [ ] hardest to fake, highest ceiling: People/Engineering Management.
- [ ] most strategic / executive-facing: Governance & Strategy.

useful test: which core question would u be happiest answering every day for five years?
thats your track.

your Goal 4 portfolio should lean toward your chosen track, but include at least one cross-track artifact.
everyone benefits from a post-mortem and a business case.
