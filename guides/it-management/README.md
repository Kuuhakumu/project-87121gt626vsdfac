> [!IMPORTANT]
> **read this first: "IT Manager" is a destination, not an entry point.**
> almost no one gets hired straight into IT management with zero experience.
> u manage budgets, people, vendors, and risk. organizations hand that authority to people who have earned credibility by doing the work first.
>
> realistic on-ramps: Service/Ops, Project, Technical/Architecture, or Business/MBA.
> this guide builds the skills and artifacts those on-ramps require.
> treat it as a multi-year career arc u can start preparing for now, not a 3-month sprint into a manager title.

## certification reality in 2026

```text
[Entry signal]    ITIL 4 Foundation: most recognized ITSM baseline, no prereqs
[Project track]   CAPM / CompTIA Project+: entry project-management signal
[Agile shops]     PSM I / PSPO I: Scrum vocabulary, lifetime-valid
[Governance]      COBIT Foundation: intro to IT governance
[Architecture]    TOGAF: enterprise architecture / strategy track
[Skip early]      CGEIT / CISM / PMP: require years of experience
```

certs prove vocabulary, not capability to manage.
hiring managers weight judgment, communication, and track record over certificates.
ITIL 4 Foundation is the one cheap, near-universal baseline worth considering early.
the rest are strategic and track-specific.
